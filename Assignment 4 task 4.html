Bugatti

Bugatti, a name that resonates with luxury, performance, and exclusivity, is a French luxury automobile brand that has produced some of the most iconic and high-performance cars in automotive history. Founded by Italian-born French automobile designer Ettore Bugatti in 1909, Bugatti has consistently set benchmarks for speed, design, and engineering excellence.

Ettore Bugatti established the brand in Molsheim, Alsace (then part of the German Empire), with a vision to create vehicles that combined artistic design with superior engineering. The early Bugatti models, such as the Type 13 and Type 35, became legendary in the racing world, securing numerous victories and establishing the brand's reputation for performance and reliability.After a period of dormancy, Bugatti was revived in the late 20th century by Italian entrepreneur Romano Artioli, who introduced the EB110, a car that brought the brand back into the spotlight. In 1998, Volkswagen Group acquired Bugatti, ushering in a new era of automotive excellence with the introduction of the Veyron in 2005.
